Day 1 - Warm-up

My definition of self-confidence is how firmly you believe in your ability to execute a physical skill or perform a task. That’s right--confidence is how much YOU believe in YOUR ability. Confidence is derived from a baseline assessment of past performances, training, and preparation.

As your competency or skill mastery grows, your confidence becomes proportionately stronger. You can also derive confidence from the belief that you are physically talented, which mirrors the definition of confidence—“A feeling of assurance, especially of self-assurance” (Source: Dictionary.com).

Successful training and quality practice both help to create a strong sense of selfconfidence.

Day 1 - Confidence Clip

When asked about his confidence, 2005 Indy 500 Champion, Dan Wheldon stated that his confidence comes from many areas in racing, not just from a belief in his ability.

He also said that confidence comes from the people around you and how well your race equipment is functioning. In sports, such as auto racing, the performance of the car can either improve or detract from the racers level of confidence.

“Certainly confidence not only comes from yourself, I think it comes from the people around you and their attitude towards you. It also comes from how your car is acting. Certainly when you have a lot of confidence you can drive the car more on the edge than perhaps someone who is not confident.” ~Dan Wheldon, 2005 Indianapolis 500 Champion

Day 1 - Energize Your Confidence

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “I am in control of my level of confidence and how I gain confidence.”
  2. “I choose to be confident in my ability regardless of external conditions or amount of luck.”
  3. “No matter what others say or do – my success is the outcome of my actions.”
