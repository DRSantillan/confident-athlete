Day 8 - Warm-up

Confident athletes have a positive self-image and grasp positive labels to describe their ability. Boxing great, Muhammad Ali, was one of the best athletes in terms of creating an air of supremacy through positive self-labels, in regard to his performance and ability. He would say, “I am the greatest,” and he believed it! He turned many people off because they interpreted his behavior as arrogant or too cocky.

You don’t have to go as far as Ali and tell others how great an athlete you are, but if you can hold the belief—that is what is most important—you’ll view yourself as a great athlete. Envisioning yourself as a great athlete is what separates great athletes from good ones.

Day 8 - Daily Objective

To develop a healthy sports self-concept that supports strong selfconfidence.

Day 8 - Daily Confidence Boost

The goal is to identify negative self-labels and replace these with self-labels or beliefs that support your confidence, such as, “I am a winner.”

  1. Write down three positive self-labels that positively describe you in your sport, which you can truly believe. Examples: “I’m a winner” or “I’m the fastest”.
  2. Post these self-descriptions on a mirror, your refrigerator or on your desktop where you will see them every day.
  3. Strive to identify and discard any negative labels that others have given to you or that you have made up yourself; write them on a piece of paper, crumple the paper, and throw it away with authority.
  4. When speaking to others about yourself and your abilities, use the positive self-labels you have formulated with confidence but avoid deliberate displays of outward cockiness, because people who lack confidence, sometimes resent and do not understand outward displays of confidence, which they often interpret as arrogance.
  5. Goal: Feeling cocky on the inside, while being humble on the outside.

Day 8 - Energize Your Confidence

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “Perform like a butterfly – sting like a bee – I choose to perform with grace and power and be all that I can be.”
  2. “I am the best in this event because my performance in practice and competition supports this belief.”
  3. “I choose not to focus on labels that others give me. I choose to see myself as a focused and mentally tough competitor.”

Day 8 - Taking Confident Action

Write a list of 5 or 6 positive self-descriptors you can recite, that you can honestly believe in, that will support self-confidence. If you do not know where to begin, it may help to start by using positive labels or comments others have given you. What are the positive descriptions others have given you about your game or performance?
