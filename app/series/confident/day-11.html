Day 11 - Warm-up

Do you become rattled by unanticipated events that happen during competition? Confident athletes do not lose composure when faced with uncertain challenges in competition. I teach my students to anticipate as many challenges as possible, based on what has happened in the past and address their major fears.

Some regular and unusual challenges to anticipate may include difficult weather conditions, unfair officiating, rude spectators, psych-out attempts from competitors, poor play, bad luck, unexpected equipment problems and any other distractions you may encounter. Being mentally prepared to cope with any distraction or challenge, rather than viewing them as inhibiting obstacles, is very important to ensure your confidence in the upcoming competition.

Day 11 - Daily Objective

To learn how to anticipate distractions before they happen by planning a coping response.

Day 11 - Daily Confidence Boost

Use the four step process below to help you anticipate and cope with potential trouble situations.

  1. Think back to when you got sidetracked by unanticipated distractions during competition.
  2. Prepare a written list of those distractions.
  3. Record the situation, the distraction and your reaction to the event.
  4. Formulate and write down a coping response you could use to react more appropriately in case the same distraction presents itself in the future. How will you react to distracting events in a way that helps you play on with focus, confidence and composure?

Example Coping Strategy

  • Distraction:A fan in the stands starts to ride you when you are up to bat and you change your focus to his heckling.
  • Situation:You are up to bat with 2 men on base.
  • Coping action:You notice that you are focusing on unimportant cues to getting a hit. You decide to ask yourself what do you need to think about right now to have a successful at bat? You know that you must dig in and focus intensely on the pitcher and picking up the ball.

Day 11 - Energize Your Confidence

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “Nothing will rattle my composure because I am ready for anything.”
  2. “I have prepared myself to handle any adversity that comes my way.”
  3. “I choose to stay composed and confident in crunch-time.”

Day 11 - Taking Confident Action

You can also practice your coping plan by using mental imagery. Think back to the time when you had something unexpected happen to you and it became a distraction. In your mental imagery, use your coping reaction to regain your composure or refocus.
