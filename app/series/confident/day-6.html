Day 6 - Warm-up

Unrealistic or irrational thinking is an area that I pay close attention to when working with athletes, because these irrational beliefs will limit your success in sports. You can think as confidently as you want, but if you harbor negative self-labels or strict expectations, it won’t matter how much positive thinking you do.

I concentrate on uncovering beliefs that athletes unknowingly embrace, which limit success. Some of these beliefs are often subtle, irrational or unrealistic. You must be able to identify negative selflabels that undermine your confidence level.

The following are four common categories of unhealthy beliefs that can have a negative impact on your self-confidence. Your task today is to do some soul searching to identify the beliefs that sabotage your confidence:

  1. Strict expectations (“I can’t make any mistakes if I want to win”).
  2. Negative self-labels (“I’m a poor finisher”).
  3. Over-generalizations (“I don’t do well on this course”).
  4. Other unhealthy or irrational beliefs (“I need to be perfect”).

Day 6 - Daily Confidence Boost

This is another awareness exercise for your plan of ultimate confidence. The goal is to answer the following questions about your sport:

  1. What expectations, such as “I have to finish in the top 5 today,” do you place on your performance if you do not achieve your expectations? Will you become upset, frustrated, or lose confidence in your ability?
  2. What strict demands do you maintain about your performance such as, “I must come in the top five this event?”
  3. What names or negative labels do you adopt that other people give you, which are NOT helping your confidence, such as “I’m a loser and choke in big events?”
  4. What situations from the past cause you to make false generalizations about your current situation such as, “I never perform well on fast tracks?”
  5. What unhealthy or irrational beliefs do you hold onto that are not grounded in reality or cannot be observed to be true by others, such as, “I must wear my lucky headband to have the best chance at winning today?”

Day 6 - Energize Your Confidence

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “My goal for today’s competition is to compete to the best of my ability and have no expectations.”
  2. “Today is an opportunity for me to break through and look to success in spite of my past performance.”
  3. “I choose to see myself as a confident and successful athlete.”
