Day 4 - Warm-up

You, and only you, are responsible for your confidence level on game day! Remember, you create your own confidence destiny. Do not let your desire for immediate results (such as getting the first hit of the game or making your first solid par of the round) or the past dictate how confident you feel. I’m sure you have been developing confidence for several years!

Some athletes don’t take full responsibility for their own confidence prior to competition—they unknowingly wait until the game starts before they make a judgment about how confident they should feel. If this sounds like you, you must have positive results before you can feel confident, change and become proactive with your confidence.

Confidence should be based on years of practice and competition, not on your immediate performance. You have lots of successes to draw upon even before the competition begins.

Day 4 - Daily Objective

To learn how to become proactive and take control of your confidence prior to competition.

Day 4 - Daily Confidence Boost

Do not let yourself be a victim of circumstances that are beyond your control. Bring confidence to competition by:

  • Reviewing your confidence resume before competition.
  • Identify doubts and rebut or refute those doubts using the technique I teach you in Day 10 – Reframing doubts.
  • Focus on what you can control in your environment and don’t let the areas you can’t control influence your mindset.

Day 4 - Energize Your Confidence

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “I choose to be confident prior to competition and control my own destiny.”
  2. “I will use my pregame preparation to get into a confident state of mind.”
  3. “I look forward to creating my own momentum today.”

Day 4 - Taking Confident Action

  • Do not wait until you hit that first good shot, score a goal, or win the first game in the set to have confidence. Take control of your own confidence before you begin.
  • Choose to focus on those areas in your game which you can control and choose not to pay attention to what you cannot control.
