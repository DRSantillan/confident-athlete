Day 13 - Warm-up

You must start with the realization that most intimidation in sports is selfinduced. Yes, other athletes will sometimes use direct intimidation or play head games with you, but you can make the choice to not pay attention and look the other way.

However, you can’t “look the other way” when you are your own worst enemy because you are intimidated by your own thoughts about the level of the competition, the weather conditions, or the venue you play. Athletes who lack confidence often look for others to help them feel confident. Likewise, these same athletes intimidate themselves by paying too much attention to other competitors or by putting other competitors on a pedestal.

Day 13 - Daily Objective

To learn how to avoid self-induced intimidation so you can keep your confidence.

Day 13 - Daily Confidence Boost

Most self-induced intimidation comes from you giving too much energy to other competitors by making comparisons, thinking too much about the reputation of your competitors, or feeling like you do not belong at your current level of play.

  1. Avoid putting other athletes on a pedestal, as if they are better than you or superior.
  2. Stop making comparisons to athletes who you think are better than you.
  3. Focus on your strengths instead thinking about the reputation of other competitors and how you stack up.
  4. See yourself on equal ground in terms of your ability.

Day 13 - Energize Your Confidence

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “There is only one type of intimidation-- self-intimidation. I choose to focus on my world and not compare myself to others.”
  2. “I do not focus on other competitors because I will remain focused on my performance and my strengths as an athlete.”
  3. “I see myself on equal ground with my competitors. I have earned the right to perform with confidence.”

Day 13 - Taking Confident Action

Remember, you are the one telling yourself other athletes are making you feel intimidated. You will not feel intimidated when you fuel your own confidence, feel like you belong and deserve to compete at your level, and stay focused on your business in the pregame warm up.
