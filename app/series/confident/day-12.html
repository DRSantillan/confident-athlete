Day 12 - Warm-up

Errors are just that—errors, but they can turn into a monster if you let them. Some of the athletes I work with focus too much energy on errors and this causes a confidence decline. In addition to hurting their confidence, dwelling on errors makes them frustrated and causes, at the very least, athletes to focus on what has happened in the past, rather than paying attention to the present.

Everyone makes errors in sports – even the top athletes in the world make mistakes. The fact that a simple error was made – hit a bad shot, chose the wrong pitch, or ran the wrong play - is not a big deal. The big problem, from a mental game perspective, is how the athlete reacts to and thinks about the error. We know that dwelling on the error will not help. So, one of the goals is to be able to accept and let go of errors quickly so the error does not compound itself.

Day 12 - Daily Objective

To learn how to let go of errors quickly instead of letting the errors snowball into a distraction and compound the error.

Day 12 - Daily Confidence Boost

The fact that you made an error does not automatically mean that you should become upset or dwell on the error. Instead, it’s your belief or how you perceive the error that is critical to how you will ultimately react in that situation.

Therefore, you have to identify the belief or thought that is keeping you stuck in the past or making the error larger than life in your mind. The goal is to change the belief that is causing you to dwell in the past or stay stuck.

  1. List the top 5 errors you have committed recently that cause you to dwell or become frustrated.
  2. Look at each error individually, what belief (or thought about the error) caused you to feel frustrated or dwell on the error?
  3. Develop a refocus statement to help you stop dwelling on errors and remain confident.

Use table 9 on the next page to help you complete this exercise.

Example Coping:

  • Error: Three-putted from 20 feet.
  • Corresponding Belief:“I should never three-putt from this distance; what am I doing wrong with my touch today?”
  • Refocus Statement:“Even the best putters three-putt occasionally. Let it go, it’s over. Just make sure you read the speed of the putt better next time.”

Day 12 - Energize Your Confidence

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “I will stay composed when I make errors and learn from them.”
  2. “The past is in the past. I will not let what I cannot change influence my attitude and confidence.”
  3. “I will use the three R’s (Recognize-Regroup-Refocus) to help me refocus when I start to dwell in the past.”

Day 12 - Taking Confident Action

I use a simple technique to help my students refocus after errors. It’s called the three R’s for refocusing. You can use the Three R’s to help you refocus or regroup after errors or when distracted:

  1. Recognize – the fact that you are dwelling in the past.
  2. Regroup– to use a refocus statement to jerk yourself out of the past.
  3. Refocus– get focused on the next play, shot, or routine.
