Day 5 - Warm-up

A pregame or precompetition warm up routine is essential for your confidence development. Athletes at every level use pregame routines or rituals prior to their performance. However, the pregame routine or ritual is more than just physical preparation – it is a great time to become mentally prepared for a competition. Y

our mental preparation should include three things: (1) to be proactive with your confidence, (2) to prepare to focus on the process, and (3) to trust the training you have done.

Day 5 - Confidence Clip

“I go into a cocoon (of sorts) for 15-20 minutes before each game. This is my quiet time, my preparation time. I focus on the pitcher and how he will try to strike me out. I envision getting a hit off the pitcher.”~Wade Boggs, MLB Player

Day 5 - Daily Objective

To establish a precompetition routine that helps you take control of your confidence.

Day 5 - Daily Confidence Boost

Write out a plan for your precompetition routine that incorporates mental preparation and confidence conditioning strategies. Include the following six mental strategies into your pregame routine:

  1. Set aside or “park” any bothers or distractions from your life.
  2. Review your confidence resume.
  3. Sever doubts and other negative thoughts before they fester.
  4. Set a game plan or strategy.
  5. Visualize your assignments or performance in the competition.
  6. Commit to your game plan or strategy.

Day 5 - Energize Your Confidence

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “I choose to park any distractions before I compete by putting them in a small box and placing the small box on a shelf.”
  2. “I am prepared, focused, and ready to compete in today’s event.”
  3. “I use my pregame or precompetition routine to help me feel confident, focused, and ready to trust myself.”

Day 5 - Taking Confident Action

  • Practice your pregame routine before you apply it to your next competition.
  • Modify your pregame routine as needed for ultimate confidence.
