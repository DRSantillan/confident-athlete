Day 2 - Warm-up

For some athletes, confidence can be fragile and vary depending on immediate results and beliefs or the self-talk they maintain prior to or during competition. One of the goals of your confidence program is to not let immediate results (or lack of) affect your current self-confidence. This requires that you understand the thoughts, doubts, or beliefs that undermine confidence.

Self-doubt is the opposite of confidence and kills your momentum. You must reframe your negative thoughts by thinking of the bigger picture. Take confidence from the fact that your past experiences of success far outweigh a momentary feeling about doubt in your ability.

Day 2 - Confidence Clip

''I lost my confidence; I got to the point where I didn't even want to be out there, because I was playing so poorly…. You know those laminated woods? I always make the analogy that confidence is like those layers. You keep chipping away at it. You play poorly, another layer is gone. And when you're on that negative spiral, it's hard to put another layer back because there's no glue left.'' ~Ian Baker-Finch, 1991 British Open Champion

Day 2 - Daily Objective

Day 2 - Daily Confidence Boost

Make a list of the doubts and negative thoughts you maintain about your sport including the subtle questions you ask yourself about your own ability.

Below is a list of some areas to help you develop your list:

  • Self-doubt about your ability to beat your competitors.
  • Questioning the quality of your training.
  • Self-doubt about your physical ability or level of fitness.
  • Questioning the quality of your equipment (i.e. car, motorcycle, bike).
  • Self-doubt about your teammates ability.
  • Worry about not playing well in certain weather or course conditions.
  • Questioning the quality of your coaching.
  • Thoughts related to fear of failure.
  • Worry about making costly mistakes.
  • Doubts about your strategy.
  • Making comparisons to competitors who you think are better than you.
  • Feeling that you can never reach your expectations.
  • Dwelling on past failures.
  • Beating yourself up or criticizing yourself.

Day 2 - Energize Your Confidence

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “I am in control of my thoughts and abilities to recognize my own doubts.”
  2. “There is no such thing as a negative person, only negative thoughts.”
  3. “I am my own best friend and choose to let go of the past and accept that mistakes are a part of sports.”
