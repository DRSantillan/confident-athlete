Day 9 - Warm-up

Every person carries on an internal dialogue, which is known as “self-talk”. Your self-talk can be positive—“I will play well today”— or negative— “I hope I don’t embarrass myself and lose the game today.” You want to bring awareness to any negative self-talk you maintain after you make a mistake.

Why do some athletes use positive self-talk and pick themselves up after a mistake, while other athletes beat up on themselves for not performing up to expectations? Positive athletes are aware that they must adopt a mentality that supports confidence instead of reinforcing doubt.

This is really important—positive athletes develop mindsets that support confidence. You can also be proactive with your confidence by maintaining positive self-talk before, during AND after performance.

Day 9 - Daily Objective

To develop a healthy self-talk to help you be proactive with your confidence and to fight the tendency to engage in negative self-talk after mistakes.

Day 9 - Daily Confidence Boost

Below are five steps to identify the correct positive self-talk to use prior to your performance. You can use this at any time before you compete, such as before a gymnastics routine or during your performance, such as when you are on a break away in hockey.

  1. Think back to the last time you performed your best and were in the zone.
  2. What was your self-talk like? What things did you think about or tell yourself about your performance?
  3. Now, develop a list of positive statements from your best performance, such as, “You are the best competitor today.”
  4. Write these statements below.
  5. Use your mental rehearsal to experience yourself in your next competition by frequently repeating positive statements.

Day 9 - Energize Your Confidence

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “I look forward to each new day because I choose to believe I add value to the team.”
  2. “I choose to have supreme confidence on the inside and act humble on the outside.”
  3. “I choose to keep my self-talk positive and confidence-enhancing.”
