Day - Warm-up

The difficultly in writing a workbook that pertains to all sports is that I have to write broadly enough to cover many sports and sports skills. The purpose of Day 8 is to give you specific strategies for refocusing and concentrating your best, related to self-paced and continuous type sports. I cannot cover all sports here, but I can give you some advice on how to refocus for skills that are similar to the ones you perform.

Some sports are very fast paced (hockey, soccer, auto racing, racquetball) and require you to refocus quickly or you might lose the game, match, or race, or at least, compound an error. Other sports such as bowling, golf, and baseball, are not fast paced and give you time to refocus because of a natural stop-and-go action that happens between shots or pitches. These sports are called self-paced tasks because the action is paced by the performer, not by another player or response to a specific action (e.g. starting gun, whistle, tip off, etc).

In fast paced sports that do not have natural breaks in the play, but instead have a continual action, the task of refocusing is both more critical and harder to do at the same time. However, in self-paced sports, you have a lot of time to process your next or last shot and use The Three R’s prior to execution.

In addition, most self-paced sports skills allow you the opportunity to stop your preshot, pre-pitch, or pre-service routine and start over or restart the routine from an earlier point in the routine. You want to make sure you are completely ready to hit a shot, serve or throw the pitch before you execute.

Day - Daily Focus Boost

Let’s start with self-paced sports tasks that are performed in golf, tennis, bowling, free-throws, penalty kicks, and any other tasks you control when the action starts. If you play these sports or another self-paced task, you have the luxury to not begin the serve or shot until you are completely focused and ready (within the rules of the game of course). If you can learn how to use the extra time to refine your focus you’ll find it an asset. However, if you spend your time unwisely by investing it into over-analyzing or over-thinking the performance, you won’t perform your best.”

With sports that have non-stop action or are continuous (none or very little breaks in action) such as auto racing, hockey, swimming, running, or soccer, you can lose precious seconds with just one distraction. One simple mental slip caused by a distraction in a race car can cause you to wreck the car or lose valuable seconds from lap times. Continuous-action sports require that you are completely focused during competition. And if you do become distracted, you must learn to refocus very quickly – your life may literally depend on it!

With self-paced sports or tasks, you have two options for refocusing:

  1. Restart your routine
  2. Refocus quickly “on the fly”

With continuous-action sports, in most cases, you only have one option for refocusing:

Refocus quickly “on the fly”, or in real-time

Let’s see how both refocusing strategies work using specific examples. You will need to make the application to your sport based on the examples below:

Refocus Routine for Self-Paced task (Basketball Free Throw)

  1. Recognize – You sight someone behind the basket trying to distract you on purpose while you are about to hit the shot.
  2. Regroup – say to yourself “Stop – wrong thing to focus on.” You decide to restart your preshot routine from the ball bounce.
  3. Refocus – You re-engage with your preshot routine from the ball bounce and stay focused on the target only.

Refocus Routine for Continuous Sports (Triathlon)

  1. Recognize – In the mass start, your swim goggles get knocked off or hit so they are flooded with water inside.
  2. Regroup – Focus on moving to the outside, where you can put them back on or get the water out of your eyes.
  3. Refocus – Focus on getting back into a rhythm with your swim stroke. It’s a long race, you can get the time back on the bike or run.

Day - Affirm Your Focus

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “No matter what happens, I can refocus quickly and effortlessly.”
  2. “I have the discipline to stop my routine and restart my mental engine.”
  3. “I deserve to take my time by restarting my routine to help me refocus.”

Day - Taking Focused Action

  • Practice your refocus on the fly for continuous action sports, both in practice and in competition.
  • For self-paced sports, force yourself to restart your mental routine when you become distracted at least 6 times during a game or round.
