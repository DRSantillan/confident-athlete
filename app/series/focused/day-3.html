Day - Warm-up

Every person who plays sports has the ability to concentrate – you would not be able to throw a ball to a target or hit a golf shot unless you were able to focus your attention for a few seconds on an object, feeling, or target. We would go crazy if we did not have the ability to selectively attend to certain thoughts or perceptual cues in the environment. Every athlete has the power to selectively attend to certain performance “cues” and that gives you the ability to concentrate, by choosing what you focus on during your performance.

The mind works in a way to filter out what is necessary to focus on and what is not necessary to focus on in order to throw a baseball or hit a slap shot. I call these “task-relevant performance cues” because they are the important things you need to focus on for successful execution of any motor task.

The next step in this program is to identify the performance cues or task-cues that you must CHOOSE to attend to so you can perform your best. However, we must keep in mind that the important cues for each sport will change depending on what task you are engaged in. For example, a golfer must focus on a different set of performance cues when preparing to hit a drive, compared to hitting a putt. When I talk about task-relevant performance cues, there are many different types of “cues” you may need to attend to, such cues as thoughts, feelings, communication from teammates, targets, and cues from your surroundings, just to mention a few. (So keep a broad mind here when I discuss cues).

Day - Daily Focus Boost

The first step is to identify the certain tasks you perform in your sport. Then we can begin to set forth the performance cues that are important for you. This is a simple step that requires you to think about the skills you perform on a regular basis. If you only perform one skill like shooting, it makes your job even easier. However, in some sports, such as gymnastics and golf, you perform several different routines or different skills.

Examples of Skills Performed in Golf:

  • Hitting a tee shot/ full shot from the fairway to the green
  • Chipping a ball onto a green
  • Putting ball into a hole
  • Hitting a fairway bunker shot
  • Hitting a green-side bunker shot
  • Playing a specialty shot

Day - Affirm Your Focus

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “I choose to focus on performance cues that will help me execute (a skill).”
  2. “Performance cues are the foundation of my ability to concentrate.”
  3. “I understand what I need to attend to in my sport.”

Day - Taking Focused Action

  • In your next practice or competition, I want you to review your list of important performance cues so you know what to think about.
  • Your mission is to become immersed in the cues you have identified in your sport that will allow you to execute successfully.
