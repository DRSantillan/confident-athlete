Day - Warm-up

Professional athletes use specific pregame rituals or routines to instill a winning mindset, and you should too. The ultimate goal of your pregame routine is to create a mindset that lets you perform up to your full potential! Consistent mental preparation leads to consistent performance - the hallmark of champion athletes. Your pregame warm up is an excellent time to integrate your focusing strategies.

Think of the pregame routine as your final mental tune-up prior to competition. It has four important purposes: (1) to get loose and physically warm up, (2) to focus the mind, (3) to instill confidence, and (4) to help you trust your ability. The pregame routine is also an excellent time to transition from life into sport – and assume or enter the role of the focused athlete.

Day - Daily Objective

To learn how to use your normal pregame warm up routine to help you dial in your focus for competition.

Day - Daily Focus Boost

The 4 main objectives of the pregame routine are as follows:

  1. To be fully focused on execution. What you focus on prior to competition is critical to your mental game success. As we have discussed, you must direct your focus in a meaningful way.
  2. To feel confident in one’s skills. Confidence is the number one objective of your mental preparation. Confidence without strict expectations is my formula for success. As you will learn, confidence is built in many ways, some sources are practice, preparation, game plans and the mindsets you maintain (see “The Confident Athlete” CD Program: http://www.peaksports.com/proshop.php).
  3. To prepare to cope with adversity. In the corporate world, executives are taught to develop a crisis plan before an emergency strikes. This allows them to formulate a plan when their heads are clear, rather than trying to create a plan out of necessity when emotions are running high. Experienced athletes create coping mechanisms to deal with different forms of adversity, such as situations that could cause them to lose focus, confidence or composure. The goal here is to anticipate the challenges that might affect your ability to focus your best and develop strategies to cope with each situation.
  4. To fully enter the role of the athlete. This was covered in the last session (lesson 9). I use this phase to help athletes make the transition between life and sports.

Day - Affirm Your Focus

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “I use my pregame routine to become a focused athlete.”
  2. “I am ready to go at the start of the game, match, or race.”
  3. “I am prepared, focused, and confident at the start of the game.”

Day - Taking Focused Action

  • The first step is to apply your pre-game routine to practice. This is an excellent way to become familiar with the steps in the pre-game routine.
  • The second step is to begin to apply your pre-game routine to competition. Start with one or two of the concepts in this section first, and add more concepts as you get better.
