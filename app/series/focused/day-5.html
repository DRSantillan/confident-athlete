Day - Warm-up

Up to this point in the program, we have only talked about identifying the information that is relevant to successful performance. However, the information that is not relevant is an important step in helping you improve focus too. Many of the athletes I work with tend to overload their brains with too much information – more than they can handle at one time.

Information overload or having misleading information sends mixed signals to your body. In this indecisive state, the body will not execute with the desired outcome or rhythm. Once you define performance cues and can clearly recognize non-relevant cues or distractions, you are now in a better position to become fully immersed into your performance – an important quality of being in the zone or gaining a zone focus.

Day - Daily Objective

To learn how to immerse yourself in the process of execution and not let distractions penetrate your focus.

Day - Daily Focus Boost

The goal of becoming fully immersed into your sport or performing a skill is to keep your mind fixed on what is important- as I have discussed. One way we can accomplish immersion in the task is to have you perform a short, guided, mental rehearsal task.

Here is your task:

Find a quiet area where you will not be distracted. Close your eyes and experience yourself at your favorite place such as a beach, meadow, stream, forest, mountain or ocean. This place should be special to you, as well as peaceful and joyous. Now, I want you to take in the environment, noticing everything you can about your surroundings, such as sights, feelings, sounds, smells, and tastes, or any other information that comes to mind. Just observe your environment and what you are feeling right now. In your mind, become fully immersed into this experience of observing your favorite place.

Your mission is to stay immersed into absorbing and noticing your surroundings without getting distracted by irrelevant thoughts. If a distraction does enter your mind, let it pass and get back to experiencing your favorite place and noticing your surrounding.

If this task was easy for you to do without any internal distractions, then you have the ability to become immersed into your sports tasks. Practice this skill often if you had trouble focusing on your favorite place without distractions. You will find that the more you practice, the easier it will get to become fully immersed in your sports tasks.

Day - Affirm Your Focus

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “I can become fully focused and immersed into my performance.”
  2. “I know what I need to focus on to become fully immersed.”
  3. “Only the present moment matters, not the past or future.”

Day - Taking Focused Action

  • Throughout the course of your daily activities, I want you to notice the times when you are fully immersed into what you are doing. What does this feel like to you?
  • Notice the times during the day when you have the most trouble focusing on the task and becoming immersed into an activity. Where does your mind go when you are off-task?
