Day - Warm-up

Today is the day we put all your new focusing skills into action or practice – and I am not talking about competitive action. I am talking about using mental imagery to rehearse your focusing skills with the Focused Athlete Mental Imagery Program. How is this mental rehearsal program different than the “Affirm Your Focus” section? This is a longer mental imagery program that helps you ingrain and practice all The Focused Athlete concentration skills in a relaxed setting. Day 14 also serves as an excellent summary of the skills you have learned in the previous 13 days.

Day - Daily Objective

To review and mentally practice “The Focused Athlete” concentration skills, using mental rehearsal.

Day - Daily Focus Boost

You will need 15-20 minutes to complete The Focused Athlete Mental Imagery program. I suggest that you find a quiet spot at home or in a hotel room where you will not be disturbed for about 20 minutes. Do not listen to this guided imagery program while driving a car, as it is important that you are completely focused on the imagery program, plus it’s also important for you to drive safely! The program begins with about 5 minutes of relaxation that is followed by the guided imagery program, which will take you through a general pre-game routine and then part of your actual performance. You will have a chance to practice your refocusing skills and review your pregame routine.

This program is not specific to any sport so it applies to any competitive situation.

There are three sections in the “Focused Athlete” Mental Imagery Program:

  • Get “present focused”
  • Short relaxation
  • Guided imagery program

Please go to track 15 (Audio CD 2) now to begin The Focused Athlete Mental Imagery Program. You will need about 18 minutes to complete the Mental Imagery Program.

Day - Taking Focused Action

  • Complete the Focused Athlete Mental Imagery Program at least once per week and prior to important competition. Please use this program up to two hours prior to competition, but not right before competition as some athletes become too relaxed with the relaxation section.
