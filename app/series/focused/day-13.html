Day - Warm-up

A post-performance evaluation of your mental game helps you identify areas you need to improve and helps you stay committed to good concentration. By doing a post-performance evaluation of your focusing skills, you can modify your process goals or pre-game routine to help you improve for the next performance. You have many options to evaluate your mental game performance after a game or performance.

For example, I use post performance feedback forms to help my personal coaching students commit to and evaluate their application of mental skills to performance. For the purpose of this workbook, we will assess your ability to concentrate, refocus, and have an effective pre-game routine.

Day - Daily Objective

To be able to objectively assess your mental game performance: specifically your ability to concentrate well after a competition.

Day - Daily Focus Boost

You will need to assess your concentration skills daily after each performance. I ask all my personal coaching students to do this for all aspects of the mental game. However, here I will ask you to only focus on the skills that relate to concentration.

Thus, based on your last performance, assess the following areas of your concentration using Table 15 below:

  • Ability to “enter the role” of the focused athlete before competiton
  • Selection of process goals to focus on prior to performance
  • Ability to stay focused on process goals during performance
  • General ability to immerse yourself in the present moment
  • Ability to identify distractions or irrelevant cues and adjust
  • Ability to refocus when you are distracted or off-task
  • Ability to use a focused pre-performance routine (or pre-shot)
  • Ability to recognize when your pre-shot routine breaks down
  • Ability to make an adjustment when your routine breaks down
  • Ability to focus successfully during crunch-time

Other Questions for Assessing Your Concentration Skills:
1. What focusing skills do you need to improve for the next performance?
2. What process goals should you set for my next performance?
3. At what times did you become distracted and what was the distraction?
4. What else do you want to say about your focusing skills for this game?

Day - Affirm Your Focus

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, you will repeat the following affirmations to yourself three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “I can refine my focusing abilities to become a focused athlete.”
  2. “I choose to assess my concentration ability after each game.”
  3. “I will be a focused athlete everyday I practice and perform.”

Day - Taking Focused Action

  • After your next competition, complete “The Focused Athlete Evaluation” in Table 15 above.
  • Based on your post-performance focus test, determine what skills you need to work on in practice and for the next competition (refocusing, identifying performance cues, process goals, etc.).
