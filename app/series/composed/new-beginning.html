Congratulations! You have completed The Composed Athlete. The new, composed you is now on your way to maximum composure. You should be proud of yourself for taking action with your mental game to be the most composed athlete you can be and committed to excellence in sports. Take a moment to congratulate the efforts you have made so far!

I need you to take real action now! You are armed with the powerful information and tools to help you improve composure and cope with setbacks, which is an excellent first step! But, this is just the beginning to rocket your self-composure to new heights. Now your goal is to apply this to your sport on a daily basis.

I inform all my mental game coaching students that learning any skills, especially mental skills, is a three part process. The first part is the ability to understand the mental strategies that will help improve your mental game. The second stage is the ability to apply your mental strategies to your sport daily. The third and final stage is your ability to commit, over the long term – months and months, to improve your focus and mental attitude. Your learning is never complete without the diligence to stay committed to your mental training program.

My suggestion is to review The Composed Athlete at least once a month and more often if necessary. Use the “Scoreboard” sections at the end of each session after every performance to help you identify areas to improve. On the following page, I have included a summary of the action plans for each day of the 14-day program that will help you take action daily. Remove this page from your workbook and post it where you can see it every day. You should review these action plans often and work daily to apply 1-2 of the concepts to your practice, training, and competition!
