Day 14 - Warm-up

In today’s lesson, I want to provide you with a plan for integrating your mental strategies for composure into competition. The best way to do this is have a mental preparation plan for pre-competition. A mental preparation plan can be integrated into your regular warm up routine before competition.

I teach athletes to develop a pregame or warm up routine that includes various mental game strategies, such as focus, confidence, trust, and creating a game plan. The warm up routine or pregame routine is an excellent time for you to apply your composure strategies to competition. It’s a good time to review your strategies as well.

For optimal composure, the goal is to integrate your mental preparation strategies I have covered in this program into your pregame routine.

Day 14 - Daily Composure Boost

The best way to integrate your mental preparation for composure is to complete the following steps into your pregame routine. These can be accomplished while you are driving to the game, dressing in the locker room, stretching, or as you get warmed up for competition.

8 Pregame Steps to Maximum Composure

  1. Step 1 - Discard your expectations
  2. Step 2 - Stop the mind reading
  3. Step 3 - Strive for success
  4. Step 4 - Reinforce a positive self-image
  5. Step 5 - Break mental barriers
  6. Step 6 - Become the intimidator
  7. Step 7 - Accept a non-judgemental mindset
  8. Step 8 - Utilize proactive coping strategies
Table 18. My Pregame Routine
Pregame Step
Pregame Strategies
1. Discard your expectations
  • Identify expectations that limit you
  • Replace expectations with process goals
  • Use confidence instead of expectations
2. Stop the mind reading
  • Stop thinking about what others think about you
  • Focus on your goals instead of others’ expectations
  • Draw confidence from self, not from others
3. Strive for success
  • Set positive, growth-oriented success goals
  • Think only about what you want to achieve
  • Enter the success-driven mindset
4. Reinforce a positive self-image
  • View yourself as a composed athlete
  • Visualize yourself performing with composure
  • Be confident in your ability to execute skills
5. Break mental barriers
  • Discard preconceived notions about your capabilities
  • Think about the present instead of results
  • Know you can perform well with a lead
6. Become the intimidator
  • Do not compare your skills with others
  • Focus on your warm up and not competitors ability
  • Focus on strategy for the competition, not others
7. Accept a nonjudgmental mindset
  • Recognize that humans cannot be perfect in competition
  • Give yourself the luxury of making 2-3 errors
  • Anticipate, but don’t expect you will make mistakes
8. Utilize proactive coping strategies
  • Prepare your coping strategies to handle mistakes
  • Let go of mistakes quickly and get to the next play
  • Use mistakes to grow and improve yourself

Day 14 - Collect Your Composure

Repeat the following affirmations three times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “I use my pregame routine to help me collect my composure.”
  2. “I choose to mentally prepare myself prior to the game so I’m ready for anything.”
  3. “I have a free will and can choose how I react to adversity.”

Day 14 - Action Plans For Composure

  • Write down the steps in your pregame warm up routine based on what you typically do prior to completion. Decide on the best time to apply the 8 pregame composure steps I outlined above into your pregame routine.
  • Assess your pregame routine once a month and modify your mental preparation steps depending on what steps are most important for you to apply.
