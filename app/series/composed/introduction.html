A 14-Day Plan for Maximum Composure

Welcome to The Composed Athlete: A 14-day Plan for Maximum Composure. You cannot compete at the highest levels of performance without composure of mind. As a matter of fact, without composure you will not be able to compete at any level of your sport(s) because you will hold yourself back with self-sabotaging feelings of inadequacy, worry, doubt, and fear. These are strong terms that frequently are encountered by athletes, but composed athletes are not influenced by these kinds of mental game “ball-and-chains.”

The Composed Athlete teaches you everything I share with my individual mental coaching students when working in the area of emotional control and coping with pressure encountered by athletes. At the end of this program, you will have learned the key mental strategies to help you perform with great calmness of mind and confidence in your own self-control and ability to stay composed in crunch-time. In The Composed Athlete I provide you with a system to identify what mental breakdowns hold you back from composure, how to develop a mindset to thrive under pressure, and how to let go of mistakes that cause you to dwell in the past.

“We maintained our poise. That's part of the maturity of our team, especially on the road. We don't lose our composure, and we do the right things down the stretch.”
~ Michael Jordan, After Winning 3rd Consecutive NBA title

Your goal every time you hit the field, court, or course is to think like a champion by not letting competitors get under your skin and affect your confidence through mental preparation to cope effectively with mistakes or adversity.

Most athletes and coaches understand that composure is very important to success, yet do not know the mental triggers or beliefs that drain athletes from keeping a calm, level head in competition. Composure is a very broad term in my work with athletes and as you will soon learn; there may be other mental barriers that ultimately affect your level of composure on the playing field. For example: some athletes focus too much on having the perfect performance or looking good to others (such as teammates, parents, coaches) and these mindsets affect composure during competition. The very first step to gaining superior composure as a competitor is to look internally to uncover the ineffective attitudes that undermine your ability to hang tough in moments of adversity. Then, the next step is to apply mental strategies to overcome the mental hurdles that prevent you from having full composure.

In the nearly two decades I’ve worked with athletes from all sports and all levels, I have learned that mental skills for composure can be learned just as an athlete works at developing a perfect swing in baseball or dismount in gymnastics. The Composed Athlete teaches you how to identify your mental roadblocks to composure AND develop the mindset needed to enter competition with complete composure and steadfastness.

How to use this program

Each day you will be given specific tasks to complete to work on your composure. Sometimes you will be given awareness exercises to help you identify mindsets that either undermine or contribute to success in sports. At the end of 14 days, you should be on your way to maximum composure in your sport. Every day of the program is divided into daily tasks which you need to complete to gain the most from the program. For maximum benefit from each daily composure boost, you should complete all the tasks, starting with Day 1.

Use the audio program that accompanies The Composed Athlete to help guide you through the program. For example, you should listen to the audio for Day 1 and then complete the workbook exercises for Day 1 before proceeding to the Day 2 audio and exercises.
