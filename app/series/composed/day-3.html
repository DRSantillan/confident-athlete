Day 3 - Warm-up

Don’t get me wrong, perfectionism in sports is not all bad. In fact, perfectionism helps you work hard in practice and strive for perfection by mastering your skills and routines. However, most perfectionistic traits are not effective for helping you stay confident and composed on the playing field during competition and in addition, it has some real disadvantages.

When you don’t reach the expectations you set for yourself, it is easier to become frustrated, resulting in a loss of composure. Many perfectionistic athletes struggle with fear of failure, which I will address in Day 6. Fear of failure causes a loss of composure when under stress in competition, because you become so anxious about results or outcomes and not failing.

Day 3 - Daily Objective

To identify the disadvantages of perfectionism that lead to poor composure and learn how to perform without the constraints of perfectionism.

Day 3 - Daily Composure Boost

The first step is to identify the negative habits of perfectionism that cause you to lose emotional composure and recognize what perfectionism disadvantages you posses. The second step is to develop mental strategies to perform free without anxiety or fear of failure. What are the characteristics of perfectionism that you see in yourself when you perform or train?

Examples of Perfectionistic Traits:

  • Want to win badly, which leads to anxiety and fear of failure.
  • Anxiety and fear cause you to perform tight and scared, or afraid to make mistakes.
  • Worry too much about what other people think about your game.
  • Try too hard to be perfect with your performance.
  • Views performance as either good or bad, with no gray area.
  • Try too hard and/or train more, which leads to higher expectations.
  • Respond to mistakes with frustration and self-judgment.
  • Think too much about technique or mechanics of the sport.
  • Dwell on errors or mistakes, which leads to more frustration.
  • Worry too much about future results, instead of playing in the present.
  • Become anxious and have a need to over-control, especially in pressure situations.

Five Tips for Developing a Healthier Attitude about Your Sport

  1. Strive for perfection in practice; accept you’re human and can’t be perfect in competition.
  2. Train yourself to focus on execution (For more information on concentration, see “The Focused Athlete,” part 2 in this series of performance enhancing books) instead of thinking ahead to “what ifs.”
  3. Be mindful of placing strict expectations on your performance.
  4. Stop caring or mind reading about what others may or may not be thinking about your game.
  5. Study and train hard for competition. Then “test” your skills with freedom, trusting the skills you have enhanced with routine practice.
  6. Learn to be more accepting of mistakes and encourage the ability to move forward and focus on the next play, shot, race, or routine.
