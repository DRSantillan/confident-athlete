Taking Fearless Action – My Commitment Contract

  • I commit to mentally rehearsing what it feels like to be my composed model athlete.
  • I commit to recognizing when I am committing mental game errors and to become more aware of mental breakdowns that undermine composure.
  • I commit to accepting that I can’t be perfect as an athlete.
  • I commit to accepting that it’s OK and natural to make mistakes.
  • I commit to stop the mind reading of what I think others expect of me.
  • I commit to recognizing when I crave approval from others via my performance, and know the only opinion that matters is my own!
  • I commit to replacing my irrational thoughts about my performance with new, healthy beliefs.
  • I commit to identifying fears I hold on to that hold me back from performing to my full potential or ability.
  • I will challenge my fears and replace them with growth-oriented statements.
  • I commit to setting success-driven goals that will focus my mind on achieving what I want instead of what dwelling on the past or predicting the future.
  • I commit to identifying negative self-images and replacing them with a strong, confident self-image.
  • I commit to breaking free of any self-imposed comfort zones that hold me back.
  • I commit to being the intimidator instead of letting others intimidate me.
  • I commit to focusing only on the thoughts I can control in my sport experience.
  • I commit to note any unrealistic expectations that may leave me feeling frustrated if I don’t achieve them.
  • I commit to reacting to errors with full composure and quickly letting them go from my mind.
  • I commit to use my pre-determined coping strategies for letting go of errors on a daily basis.
  • I commit to using the pregame routine to help me develop a composed attitude.
