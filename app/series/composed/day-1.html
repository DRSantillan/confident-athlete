Day 1 - Warm-up

The first characteristic of a composed athlete is the ability to remain calm, steadfast, or in control during crunch-time or competitive pressure. The composed athlete is a “gamer” or the person who thrives under competitive pressure, such as Tiger Woods when he has the lead in a golf tournament. Athletes with composure are able to raise the level of their play during competition – they thrive when the battle is close.

The second main attribute of a composed athlete is the ability to remain composed emotionally when challenged by misfortune or after committing a mistake, such as Roger Federer’s ability to quickly let go of errors. Do the best athletes in the world get upset by mistakes? You bet! However, these athletes do not dwell on past mistakes or let mistakes compound into more mistakes. They don’t linger on frustration or lose focus and their ability to think straight. A motto that I teach all my students comes from one of my early mentors in sports psychology, Dr. Ken Ravizza:

“You first must be in control of yourself before you can control your performance.”

Some people think that either you have composure or you don’t, and that you can’t change this twist of fate. In other words, you are either born with the gift of composure or not. For example, they believe that if you are a Type A personality you can’t change the fact that you become anxious in competition. This is simply false. You can learn the mental strategies that will give you a greater sense of inner composure and confidence that will enable you to perform at your peak every time you are in competition!

Thus, in summary, composure in athletics includes the following elements:

  1. The ability to think calmly and rationally when under competitive stress.
  2. The ability to react calmly and rationally to errors during competition.
  3. The ability to rise to the challenge with confidence when called upon or as needed.
  4. The ability to identify and quickly extinguish mental barriers that undermine composure and cheat you from peak performance.
  5. The ability to “want the ball” with 1 minute left in the game with the belief that there is no better person to win than you.

As an added bonus, if you are a leader in your team and can display maximum composure, other athletes will follow your lead and have confidence in your ability to lead with poise.

Day 1 - Collect Your Composure

You will be guided through a short period of relaxation (an abdominal breathing exercise) followed by a simple guided mental imagery exercise. In addition, please repeat the following affirmations to yourself 3 times in the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “I am in control of my thoughts and thus my composure.”
  2. “Composure is a calmness of mind to act with freedom when under pressure.”
  3. “Composure is the ability to react rationally and calmly to mistakes.”

Day 1 - Action Plans For Composure

  • Your task is to spend 10 minutes a day rehearsing what it would feel like and look like when you are totally composed.
  • Based on the 10 qualities you listed in table 3, your task is to experience yourself (mentally rehearse) how you would feel and behave when you are portraying your composed model athlete.

Statements To Consider:

  1. Recall what your model athlete looks like under pressure.
  2. What behaviors does he or she display before important plays or shots?
  3. Imagine what this athlete is thinking at the time.
  4. Imagine this athlete’s reactions after errors.
  5. Experience yourself performing in your next competition.
  6. See and feel yourself performing with composure in competition.
  7. Pretend that you have the same confidence and focus under pressure.
  8. Imagine yourself letting errors just slide off your back.
  9. Feel a quiet mind and calmness of emotion under stress.
  10. How does it feel to imagine yourself performing with composure?

Can you see yourself performing with this same level of composure as your model athlete?

Can you experience yourself reacting calmly to errors and moving on?

The goal is to control the images in your mind and experience yourself performing with the same level of composure as your chosen athlete.
