Day 6 - Warm -up

Do you have a little devil or little angel on your shoulder after you make mistakes?One of the challenges you have to overcome as a profession list is learning how to cope better with errors when you were not performing well. Highly motivated athletes with Perfectionistic tendencies are particularly prone to becoming upset and frustrated with mistakes always not performing up to expectations. and analytical, self-critical attitude they feel natural to you, but I am here to tell you that you need to learn how to be your own best coach

The goal of today’s lesson is to teach you strategies to help you keep eventempered, level-headed, or calm mind, especially after mistakes.

As a perfectionistic athlete, I know you can be hard on yourself. Your internal dialogue or mental chatter, known as self-talk, can be very negative and critical, especially when you are not performing well. At any given moment, your self-talk can be positive or negative about your performance. You most likely engage in name-calling with yourself such as, “I’m a choker and can’t close the game!”

Another goal of today’s lesson is to make certain you are engaging in positive self-talk especially when not performing well or after mistakes.

Day 6 - Fearless Strategy

The first step in today’s lesson is to define your personal hot buttons. When are you most likely to become negative, beat yourself up, or engage in negative self-talk? Your personal hot buttons will be different from another athlete. I can help you improve your self-talk by giving you examples based on the most common situations I encounter in my work with athletes.

Below a typical situations where negative self talk can occur:

  • Getting off to a slow start in game performance
  • after making wanted to mistakes in a row
  • Not performing up to your own expectations
  • thinking... Here I go again... Performing poorly
  • losing a point of getting beat when you should've won
  • Needing to come through the crunch time to win a game

Day 6 - Success Mottos

Repeat the following information two times during the guided imagery exercise

  1. I choose to be my own best coach and support myself.
  2. I will only maintain positive self talk, and stop my negative self-talk.
  3. I arm myself with positive strategies to overcome my hot buttons.

Day 6 - Action Plans

  1. Continue to identify your hot buttons or situations in which you become negative or self-derogatory.
  2. Continue to develop new coping strategies to overcome negative self-talk.
