Day 9 - Fearless Strategy

The first step is to understand how fear of failure and perfectionism sabotage is your ability to perform freely(and fearlessly) in competition. let's start with the definition.The performance mindset(trust) is your ability to let go of consciously directing your body allowing you well and physical skills to happen reflexively based on what you had practised.

the concept of trust is different from self confidence. Confidence is a belief in your ability that healthy play well in competition. Trusting your skills and your abilities.Fear of failure can undermine trust in many ways:

  • Perfectionist and mentally trapped in a practice mindset and thus have a hard time Performing involuntary in competition
  • perfectionists are afraid to fail and avoid making mistakes and all costs even resorting to playing to carefully
  • Performing to carefully or defensively leads you to overcontrol your performance for technique; a lack of trust.
  • Since perfectionists think they need to perform the best, you might try too hard to be perfect with your game or performance.
  • The fear of making mistakes or disappointing others(fear of negative social scrutiny) also easier to news trust and not perform freely in competition
  • Thus, fear of failure least trying to be perfect. Trying to be perfect causes overcontrol.Over control leave the lack of trust with your performance.
  • The cycle continues as you analyse what went wrong, work harder and fixing the Errors, and then getting even more fixated in the training training mindset.
