Day 1 - Warm-up

For msny Athletes, fear and fear of failuire are a constant source of stress and anxiery that sabotages performance. What is Fear Fear is an unpleasant often strong emotion caused by anticipation or awareness of danger. In tennis, most of the fear athletes refer to is not about being in danger or harming themselves phusically. The fear I am talking about is a pyschological threat that is often based on an athletes perception of the importance of a performance or game and what others think about his or her performance.

Most of the time, an athletes fear is worry related to poor results - whether prior to or during a performance. Athletes often fear the negative consequences or their performance.

They worry about many things that aare often not under their control. Some Athletes are afraid to not perform up to their own potential after putting in so much work and effort.

Other athletes are afraid to fail and fear disappointing coaches or their parents. Other athletes perform poorly because they want to avoid embarrasment.

However, the threat athletes feel in these situations can sometimes be irrational and not based on reality. Worrying about what others think about you if you hit a bad shot is often not rational and based on false assumptions you make about others.

Day 1 - Objective

To understand common fears and anxiety in tennis - not all fear is rational or based on reality. The goal is to recognize the fear that prevents you from performing freely at your peak potential.

Day 1 - Fearless Strategy

Fear of failure is one of the major barriers to success for athletes. Understand that your fear does not emrge from an poopnent or a difficult game. Fear of failure comes from within oneself. Fear will often make you perform tense, cautious, careful and create a lack of risk taking.

Table 1 is a self test to take that measures your fear in tennis and lack of self-trust. Check the statements that you feel are true.

  • I have difficulty taking my practice game to competition
  • My level of "competitive self-confidence" does not match the level of practice self-confidence. (I feel more confident in practice than when I play competitively.)
  • I want to win or succeed badly and this leads to cautious performances because I am afraid of making mistakes
  • I focus too much on results or outcomes of my performance(such as the need for a certain score), and thus have a hard time enjoying the process.
  • I can become impatient with myself if I dont perform well in competition and want improvements in paractive ti happen faster.
  • I often feel llike my self-esteem is threatened ehen I di not perform up to my own expectations
  • I care too much about what others think and make assumptions that are not based on reality, such as my parents will not drive me to practice if I dont perform well
  • I have a fear of letting others down if I dont perform up to their expectations. For example, I wrongly assume that my team wiill not like me if i lose
  • I tighten up pr becoome tense in competition and cant perform freely.
  • I play with too much physical tension and have trouble playing relaxed and free because I try to over-control my movememts or performance
  • I try to hard to win and this get un my own way mentally with a lack of trust
  • I try to be perfect with my execution and thus get tense, try to over-control my body, or get frustrated when my game is imperfect.

Assessing your results: if you check yes to 5 or more ststements above its a good bet that fesr of failure hinders your focus in competition and causes a lack of trust. if you checked yes to one or more statements, yo may still need to address this mental game barrier as you may habe at least some perfectionistic tendencies.

What are the common types of fears for tennis players?

Its not enough to know that you have fear of failure. Whats more important is to know what ttype of fear holds you back. As you can see from the list below, fear of failure often is related to what you assume others think about you.

  • Fear of losing a match or game - you badly want to win and are afriad you will not win.
  • Fear of negative social evalution - you fear others will view you as a failure in tennis.
  • Fear of embarrasment - you are afraid to enbarrass yourself in front of others if you dont perform well.
  • Fears of letting others down - you do not want to lwt others down such as a couch or parent.
  • Fear of puttin in the effort and not ever getting the "pay off" or not playing to your potential. You dont want tour hard work, talent and long practices to result in nothing. (wins, trphiesm etc)
  • Fear of not performing up to others expectations - you worry about not meeting others expectations for you
  • Fear of being rejected, losing resoect, or not gaining approval.
  • Fear of making mistakes and not performing perfectly after having worked so hard at it.

The fist step in managing your fear of failure is to understand the specific source of your fear. By the end of this course you will overcome your fears.
