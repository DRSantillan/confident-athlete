Day 12 - Warm-up

many athletes train all year around. Ins some sports, such as skating, an athlete will spend months of trainin for one major competition of the year. skaters spend 95% of practive time in the training mindset. as discussed in previous lessons, too m uch of just thetraining mindset makes it difficult to transition into the performance mindset when competition time comes around.

In other sports like golf, perfectionists can become enamored with their gold swing or technique. They spend most of their practice time on the practive tee working to improve mechanics of the swing and very lettle time playing on the course. This scenario also makes it difficult for the golfer to transition to the performer mindset in competition. These golfers look like stars in practice, but hackers on the course.

One goal of the The Fearless Athlete is to help you perform fearlessly, without the mental traps of trying to be perfect or the fear of making mistakes. You have to learn how to play on "instinct", win ugly, perform without over-control or excess tension in competition to allow your true abilities to shine. Now you know what trust is and how you sabotage your own trust. You also learned the situations and mindsets that contrubute to a mental game that avoids mistakes. The final step of the fearless athlete is to learn mental strategies that help you transform into the performer mindset in competition.

Day 12 - Objective

To learn how to perform with unbeatable trust and fearlessness. To utilize the practice mindset and performer mindset when you practice so you can perform freely in competition.

    Taper your training as you approach competition so yu can get into the competitive mindset.

    *basebal example: 2 days before a game I will stop working on my swing in the batting cage and instead see the ball and react.

  • 2. KNow when to train and when to perform

    Train your game during practice.

    * Golf example: I will train swing mechanics only when on the practice tee.

    My sports specific task:

    Be a performer in competition.

    *golf example: When I am over the ball in competition I will use one simple swing cue or thought to execute shots.

  • 3. Simplify my performance

    Focus on only one "cue" at a time while performing.

    *Tennis example: When serving, I will only focus on the target area in the service court when I want the ball to land

    My sports specific task:

    Use images and feelings instead of instructing yourself with verbal commands.

    *Tennis example: I will focus on the feeling proper tempo or balance when hitting my serves.

    My sports specific task:

  • 4. Perform freer and less serious

    Dont treat tour sport as a "life or death" situation.

    *Baseball example: I will stay calm and composed at-bat and not worry about getting a hit.

    My sports specific task:

    strive to try less, play on instinct, and let it happen.

    *baseball example: Seeing the ball and reacting quickly is my goal at-bat. I will not make it over-complicated.

  • Be committed

    Be fully committed to your decisions such as race plan, game strategy, shot plan etc.

    *Motocross example: I will execute my race plan to ride smooth with effortless speed regardless of my position in the race.

    Increase decisiveness to increase trust - dont be wishy washy.
