Day 12 - Warm-up

Many athletes “train” all year around. In some sports, such as skating, an athlete will spend months of training for one major competition of the year. Skaters spend 95% of practice time in the training mindset. As discussed in previous lessons, too much of just the training mindset makes it difficult to transition into the performance mindset when competition time comes around.

In other sports like golf, perfectionists can become enamored with their golf swing or technique. They spend most of their practice time on the practice tee working to improve mechanics of the swing and very little time playing on the course. This scenario also makes it difficult for the golfer to transition to the performer mindset in competition. These golfers look like stars in practice, but hackers on the course.

One goal of The Fearless Athlete is to help you perform fearlessly, without the mental traps of trying to be perfect or the fear of making mistakes. You have to learn how to play on “instinct,” win ugly, perform without over-control or excess tension in competition to allow your true abilities to shine.

Day 12 - Objective

To learn how to perform with unbeatable trust and fearlessness. To utilize the practice mindset and performer mindset when you practice so you can perform freely in competition.

My Plan for Unbeatable Trust

Let’s set up your plan for unbeatable trust using the following six strategies. Your job is to make each strategy specific to your sport:

1. Focus on what you want to have happen
*Runner Example: I want to be with the runners in the front pack at the half way mark.
Taper your “training” as you approach competition so you can get into the competitive mindset.
*Baseball Example: Two days before a game I will stop working on my swing in the batting cage and instead see the ball and react.
2. Know when to train and when to perform
Train your game during practice.
*Golf Example: I will train my swing mechanics only when on the practice tee.
Be a performer in competition.
*Golf Example: When I am over the ball in competition I will use one simple swing cue or thought to execute shots. My sport-specific task:
3. Simplify my performance
Focus on only one “cue” at a time when performing.
*Tennis Example: When serving, I will only focus on the target area in the service court where I want the ball to land.
Use images and feelings instead instructing yourself with verbal commands.
*Tennis Example: I will focus on the feeling proper tempo or balance when hitting my serves.
4. Perform freer and less serious
Don’t treat your sport as a “life or death” situation.
*Baseball Example: I will stay calm and composed at-bat and not worry about getting a hit.
Strive to try less, play on instinct, and let it happen.
*Baseball Example: Seeing the ball and reacting quickly is my goal at-bat. I will not make it overcomplicated.
5. Be committed
Be fully committed to your decisions such as race plan, game strategy, shot plan, skating program, etc.
*Motocross Example: I will execute my race plan to ride smooth with effortless speed regardless of my position in the race.
Increase decisiveness to increase trust – don’t be wishy-washy.
*Motocross Example: On corners, I will stick with the plan and not deviate from it.
6. Let go of mistakes
Accept that mistakes will happen and put them behind you quickly.
Gymnastics Example:”I made a mistake with my beam routine. That’s not important now – move on.”
Avoid analyzing your mistakes and trying to fix them.
*Golf Example: Stop the analysis, move on and trust my ability to hit the next shot solid.

Day 12 - Success Mottos

Repeat the following affirmations 2 times during the guided imagery exercise.

  1. “I will use part of my practice to work on the performance mindset.”
  2. “I will train myself in practice only and be a performer in competition.”
  3. “I will simply my performance in competition and make it effortless.”

Day 12 - Action Plans

  1. Make a weekly practice plan. Identify in the practice plan how much time you will spend in the training mindset and how much time you will spend in the performance mindset. What will you do to be in the performance mindset (scrimmage, look and react, practice like you compete, etc.)?
  2. After each competition, assess how well you are able to apply the six strategies for unbeatable trust in the following section.
