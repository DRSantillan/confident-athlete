Day 5 - Warm-up

My definition of “expectation” may surprise you. I define expectations as strict demands or judgments you have about your performance. Expectations are not the same as having confidence in your ability to perform well such as having confidence in your ability to get a hit or make a lay up. Expectations are demands you place on your performance and cause you to judge how you are doing compared to how you think you SHOULD be doing.

What is the difference between “expectation” and “confidence?” Confidence is simply a belief in your ability such as “I believe I can get a hit and advance the runner on first base.” But this is not a dire need when talking about confidence – only a belief in ability to do this. Expectation is more absolute and judgmental. Expectation often comes with added pressure.

Having strict, perfectionist or unrealistic expectations can undermine your sports confidence. This is especially true when you feel that you are not meeting your own performance expectations. Essentially, high expectations set you up for failure before you even begin a competition.

In addition, expectations are usually about results such as obtaining a specific school or achieving personal statistic such as batting average. Judge yourself or your game based on these expectations. You then focus too much on outcomes, schools, or sister thinks which distract you from focusing on the process of execution. your goal is to identify and replace result expectations with simple goals that focus on execution.

Day 5 - Fearless Strategy

If you are familiar with my work, you may well know one of my formulas for success in sports. My formula is to identify and replace expectations with high confidence and manageable goals. The very first step is to flush out the expectations that cause you to pressure yourself or worry too much about results.

Below are questions that help you identify strict expectations that underlie perfectionism and fear of failure:

1. Do your “goals” for a competition focus on outcomes or results only? (Expectations can often be disguised as goals.)
2. Do you constantly judge the level of your performance based on your personal standards?
3. Do you become upset when you do not perform up to your own set of standards?
4. Do you lose confidence when you do not play to your personal standards?
5. Do you maintain expectations about the quality of your performance at the start of competition such as expecting to make zero mistakes?
