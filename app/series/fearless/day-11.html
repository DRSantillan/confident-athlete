Day 11 - Warm-up

You probably spend too much time in a practice or training mindset as discussed in the previous lesson. Therefore, one of your priorities is to learn how to stay confident and in control of yourself when your performance is not perfect or when you do not have your A game. An important lesson I teach my students is to learn how to perform efficiently instead of perfectly. I call this a "functional mindset." When you are in a functional mindset, you are more flexible mentally and can easily adapt to a less than optimal performance (when you are not perfect).

A functional mindset is the opposite of trying to make everything perfect. It starts with the idea that you DO NOT have to be perfect to perform your best. You are human and humans can't be perfect. You will make mistakes and you have to accept mistakes. Tennis coach to professional players, Brad Gilbert, calls the functional mindset "winning ugly," which he wrote a book about.

Winning ugly is the idea that you get the job done and perform efficiently. Brad Gilbert (1993) says, "I've used whatever skills I do have in a calculated way that maximizes potential; that gives me my best chance to win. It's why I've been able to beat players who are supposedly 'better' than me. You can do the same. Make the most of what you've got."

In golf, for example, winning ugly is called scrambling. A golfer finds a way to get the ball in the hole without regard for "the proper way." In today's lesson, you will learn how to adopt the functional mindset so you can play efficiently and get the job done in competition.
